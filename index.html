Introduction

Problem Statement

Financial markets can be volatile and unpredictable, with a lot of different factors that contribute to financial performance. One of the biggest obstacles in financial markets is information. As many successful traders can testify, they spend most of their time conducting research on the industry, which involves reading countless news articles and documents.

One goal for this project was to be able to cut down on the time that would be involved in conducting financial research by being able to process news headlines and provide some sort of representation. Another goal was to incorporate aspects of behavioral finance into the representation, which for this project was sentiment analysis.

For this project, I used data of top 25 daily headlines from r/WorldNews from a time frame of 8/8/2008 - 7/1/2016 to conduct sentiment analysis use that in conjunction with time-series data for the S&P 500 to train a LSTM neural network to predict volatility.

Modeling S&P500 Volatility: Time-Series and Sentiment Data

Building onto the time-series layer, I added another input layer with averages of daily headline sentiments. Sentiment polarity was measured using VADER, and then the average was taken across a 30 day window to predict if the S&P 500 would move up or down. Results were not as great, as VADER is limited in its ability to measure sentiment of articles/headlines as it is more ambiguous than measuring sentiment in reviews. News headlines can be ambiguous, which can cause results to be more inaccurate. An additional layer of text classification could be helpful in identifying relevant headlines to measure the sentiment as well.

Next Steps

Alternate Approaches

Training a Naive Bayes Classifier

Another approach I did not get a chance to fully explore was using a Naive Bayes classifier to classify articles for their relevance to a specific term or industry. However, with this approach, the topics have to be relatively narrow. Even still, there could be other factors that impact the financial markets as a whole that still would matter to specific industries. For instance, headlines detailing a trade war between the United States and China would still impact the commodities market. To effectively build a classifier, multiple layers would be needed, one for industry specific events and news and another layer for market-wide news or impacts.

Adding a BERT Text Classification Layer

For my current model, another path that I would have liked to have time to pursue is to incorporate an additional layer of text classification to only take a look at relevant headlines. I know that BERT is a very powerful tool to use for text classification purposes, and it would be incorporated into a pre-processing step which would leave only relevant headlines to be processed by the sentiment engine.

FLAIR/TextBlob/Sentiment/NewsAPI

Straight sentiment analysis on complete article text was difficult. In the beginning of this project, I had originally planned to measure sentiment of entire articles pulled using NewsAPI, but that was hindered by faulty parsers that would pick up advertisement text in additional to article text. Furthermore, sentiment of news articles isn't as straightforward as measuring sentiment in a review. It can be rather ambiguous, which makes it harder to train.

Lessons Learned

Machine Learning Concepts: NLP

From this capstone project, I learned a lot about NLP and was very interested in some of the latest research and different applications of NLP. Financial markets are very hard to model, and for a good reason, since if there was an extremely efficient model, then that would make life much simpler. However, working toward incremental improvements in modeling financial markets and identifying relationships is something I definitely want to work towards and I feel that NLP can be a very powerful tool in that.

Data Science: Web Crawling and Data Gathering

Through this project, I had my first exposure to web crawling and data collection. It's something that does not seem to be much at face value, but being able to collect data is something extremely valuable when moving towards model training and data application. I definitely plan to scope out new ways to mine and collect my own data.
